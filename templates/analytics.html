Road accidents by time

  • It is noticeable that from 2012 to +- 2016 the number of DPTs remained within the range of 250 - 550 accidents per day, but a noticeable decrease was seen in 2016 - 2020.
  • Since 2020, the number of accidents has decreased again, and rarely exceeds 100 accidents.

Road accidents by time

  • Peak Accident Hours: The hours with the highest number of car accidents are 15:00 and 16:00, with 66,399 and 63,878 accidents, respectively. These late afternoon hours appear to be the most accident-prone times of the day.

Road accidents by month

  • The winter months, like December, January, and February, see fewer accidents. Harsh weather, including snow and ice, can make driving riskier and lead to a reduction in accidents during these months.

Distribution of consequences in accidents

  • The total number of people injured in road accidents is 242.833, which indicates a significant impact on public health and safety.
  • Road accidents killed 690 people, which emphasizes the measures need to be taken to reduce the number of fatalities and improve road safety.

Collisions During Different Seasons

  • In almost all years, the number of road collisions are usually lower in Winter. This is due to reduced vehicles on the road because of the unfavourable weather conditions.

Collison Contributing Factors

  • Driver Innatention stands out the most as it is way higher compared to the other factors.


Looking for correlations

Hypothesis


Is it true, that there is a day, when it is safer to drive a car?

Let's look through the data...


  • Weekday Escalation: Accidents increased steadily from Monday to Thursday. As the workweek progresses, factors like rush hour traffic and stress may contribute to more accidents.
  • Weekend Respite: Weekends (Saturday and Sunday) had lower accident rates compared to weekdays. Reduced work-related commutes and relaxed driving behavior likely play a role.
  • Weekend Safety: Fridays saw the most accidents (141,426), while Sundays had the fewest (103,729). This suggests that people tend to be more cautious on weekends, leading to fewer accidents on Saturdays and Sundays.

Answer: Yes, it is definitely much safer to drive a car at weekend, especially on Sunday.
